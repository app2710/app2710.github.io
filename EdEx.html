Education

Academic background

Doctor of Philosophy — Chemical & Petroleum Engineering 2022 – Present (exp. Dec 2026)
University of Calgary, Calgary, AB, Canada

Thesis: Development of novel solid sorbent — geopolymer–zeolite composite — for CO₂ capture from cogeneration power plants. Supervised by Prof. Nader Mahinpey.

  • Synthesized and characterized geopolymer–zeolite composite (GZC) sorbents via in-situ crystallization of zeolite 13X within geopolymer matrices.
  • Improved CO₂ uptake from 3.2 to 4.7 mmol g⁻¹ (~30%) with stability retained over 20 adsorption–desorption cycles.
  • Characterized materials using XRD, XRF, SEM, BET surface area, BJH pore volume analysis.
  • Performed fixed-bed breakthrough experiments with MKS RGA mass spectrometer; fitted Yoon–Nelson and Thomas models.
  • Developed kinetic and diffusion models (Avrami, LDF, Thiele modulus, Weisz–Prater criterion, Arrhenius).
  • Built Aspen Adsorption models for TSA feasibility and scale-up sensitivity at 100 MWe NGCC plant.
  • Contributed to COSIA- and NSERC-funded proposals supporting over $400k in awarded research funding.
  • Named first inventor on U.S. Provisional Patent No. 63/931,682.
