About

PhD researcher in CO₂ capture & solid sorbents

Research focus

I develop geopolymer–zeolite composite (GZC) sorbents for post-combustion CO₂ capture from natural gas combined-cycle (NGCC) flue gas. My work spans synthesis, characterization, adsorption kinetics, fixed-bed breakthrough experiments, and techno-economic analysis.

Current work

  • In-situ crystallization of zeolite 13X within geopolymer matrices
  • Temperature-swing adsorption (TSA) fixed-bed cycle optimization
  • Kinetic & diffusion modeling (Avrami, LDF, Thiele/Weisz–Prater)
  • Techno-economic analysis at 100 MWe NGCC plant scale

Affiliations

  • Supervised by Prof. Nader Mahinpey

Graduate coursework

Key result

Increased CO₂ uptake from 3.2 → 4.7 mmol g⁻¹ (~30% improvement) in geopolymer–zeolite composite sorbents, with performance retained over 20 adsorption–desorption cycles under industrially relevant NGCC flue gas conditions.
